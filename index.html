Epilogue

Consciousness of Death as Spiritual Practice

“So that’s what it was!” — The final words of Ivan Ilyich

We began this journey thirteen weeks ago with desire — that holy restlessness that refuses to be satisfied with surface living. We’ve walked through deserts and discovered guides in darkness, faced threshold guardians and been anointed for purposes larger than ourselves. We’ve learned to make friends with emptiness, to find nourishment in unlikely places, and to recognize the sacred architecture within our own souls.

Now we come to the end, which is also a beginning.

Ivan Ilych’s Revolutionary Discovery

Tolstoy’s Ivan Ilyich spent most of his life avoiding the very questions that would have made his life meaningful. Only in his final days, facing the reality he’d spent decades denying, did he discover what he’d been seeking all along: not the absence of mortality but the presence of love that transcends it.

As he lies dying, he reviews his life and realizes: “Maybe I did not live as I ought to have done... But how could that be, when I did everything properly?” This is the crisis of the unlived life — the discovery that we can do everything “correctly” according to society’s standards while missing the essential thing: authentic existence.

“Ivan Ilych’s life had been most simple and most ordinary and therefore most terrible.”
— Leo Tolstoy

But Tolstoy doesn’t leave us in despair. In Ivan’s final hours, something extraordinary happens. Through intense suffering and honest self-examination, he breaks through. He stops lying to himself. He stops maintaining the facade. And in that moment of radical honesty, he experiences a profound transformation.

Tolstoy writes: “He sought his former accustomed fear of death and did not find it. ‘Where is it? What death?’ There was no fear because there was no death. In place of death there was light.”

Your Journey’s End Is a Beginning

Like Ivan Ilych, you may have started this 13-week journey believing you were seeking something outside yourself — wisdom, peace, God, enlightenment. But the great discovery, the one that transforms everything, is simpler and more radical:

The light you’ve been seeking has been within you all along.

Every archetype you’ve encountered — the Seeker, the Pilgrim, the Shadow, the Warrior, the Mystic, the Healer, the Lover, the Creator, the Wise Person, the Teacher, the Judge, the Sovereign — lives within you. They are not stages to pass through but dimensions of your full humanity to integrate.

What dies in this journey is not you, but your false self — the carefully constructed persona that, like Ivan’s “correct” life, never truly lived. What emerges is your authentic self, capable of saying with Ivan in his final moments: “Yes, it was all not the right thing... but that’s all right.”

The New Exodus

Moses led about 600,000 Hebrews and other slaves out of Egypt through a desolate wilderness to a promised land; as the biblical story goes, only two people (Joshua and Caleb) made it into the promised land from the Exodus generation. Moses was a man familiar with death, and died just before entering the promised land on his 120th birthday (Dt. 31:2). Christensen (1999) and other Hebrew Bible scholars see this association with 120 years as a riddle associated with the theme of Psalm 90 (the only Psalm attributed to Moses as author) and the human lifespan limitation mentioned in Genesis Chapter 6; Moses was, after all, the only person said to have died at 120 years in the entire Torah, also known as “The Book of Moses.” About 1,400 years later the Gospel of Luke refers to the path Christ opens as a “New Exodus,” transformation of death itself through God’s plan of deliverance.

Whether your faith tradition involves this New Exodus or another path, this Soul Encounters journey can help cleanse our perceptions from unconscious living into conscious mortality, and beyond into a transformed consciousness of death being swallowed up in life. Like the seed of grain in John’s Gospel that ‘dies’ so it may produce much wheat, this journey reveals that what appears to end actually multiplies and transforms.

Here’s a beautiful realization along the journey: the promised land is not so much a geographic destination as a life-giving relation — the eternal relationship between God and humanity that Christ’s redemption opens. Other faith traditions, of course, frame this renewed relationship with the Divine in their own ways. The freedom to realize and to live in this divine relationship, where even death becomes a doorway to greater life, is an important take-away from your Soul Encounters journey.

The Spiral Path Continues

This completion is not an ending but a commencement. You might:

  • Begin Again: Journey through the 13 weeks with deeper awareness
  • Go Deeper: Spend a full month with each archetype
  • Guide Others: Share this path with fellow seekers
  • Create Your Own: Design new practices from what you’ve learned
  • Simply Live: Embody the wisdom in daily life
“The path is a spiral; we have already climbed many steps.”
— Hermann Hesse

Ivan Ilych’s final revelation was not that he was dying, but that he had never truly lived. Your revelation may be the opposite: that in learning to live authentically, you have already transcended the very death you feared.

Blake’s Final Gift

“If the doors of perception were cleansed everything would appear to man as it is, Infinite.”
— William Blake

Jung wrote a brilliant short book titled “Modern Man in Search of a Soul” (1933) as a fascinating discussion of how humans experience a ‘loss of soul’ or self-alienation due to subtle forces that impinge on human personality over time. Where is the road back? Each journey begins by feeling our way around for that elusive, truest and most fully alive center of our being — the soul (some traditions call this ‘the image of God’ within). And when found, an experience of the sacred or “God” is often close by. May your Soul Encounters journey help bring everything — your joys and sorrows, your questions and certainties, your life and your death — to you in a new light. Revisiting your Soul Encounters practices, getting deeply into an inspiring song or creative activity — let these continue to “cleanse the doors of perception” through the years.

Congratulations!

You have completed the Soul Encounters journey.
